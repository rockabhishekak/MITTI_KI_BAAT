HIGH ALERT: PEST OUTBREAK

Fall Armyworm

(Spodoptera frugiperda)

An invasive lepidopteran pest that causes significant damage to maize and other cereal crops. Early detection is paramount for effective management and yield preservation.

SPECIMEN ID

Primary Host: Maize (Zea mays). Secondary Hosts: 80+ plant species.

Key Symptoms

01 Window Pane Holes

Early stage larvae feed on one side of the leaf, leaving a translucent membrane.

02 Ragged Leaf Edges

Older larvae consume large portions of leaves, leaving characteristic jagged patterns.

03 Frass Accumulation

Sawdust-like fecal matter visible in the whorls of the maize plant.

Economic Damage

Untreated infestations can result in yield losses of up to 40-60%. The larvae migrate to the ear/cob in later stages, directly destroying the grain and inviting secondary fungal infections.

YIELD RISKCritical GROWTH PHASEVegetative

Chemical Control

Recommended Actives

Emamectin Benzoate, Spinetoram, Chlorantraniliprole.

Apply during early morning or late evening when larvae are most active. Ensure direct contact with the plant whorl.

Organic Control

  • Introduce natural predators like Trichogramma wasps.
  • Apply Neem oil extracts or Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) sprays.
  • Intercropping with Desmodium (Push-Pull strategy).
